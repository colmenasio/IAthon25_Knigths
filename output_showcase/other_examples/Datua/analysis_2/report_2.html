Streaming Service User Behavior Analysis Report

Introduction

This report summarizes the analysis of a dataset that provides insights into user behavior, content consumption, and performance metrics for a streaming service. The dataset includes various factors such as user demographics, content genres, device usage, and ratings, which can be leveraged for strategic marketing decisions.

Dataset Overview

The dataset comprises 6,214 entries with the following columns:

  • User_ID: Unique identifier for the user
  • Session_ID: Unique identifier for the user's session
  • Device_ID: Identifier for the user's device
  • Video_ID: Identifier for the video content being viewed
  • Duration_Watched (minutes): Time spent watching the video
  • Genre: Genre of the video content (e.g., Action, Comedy, Drama, etc.)
  • Country: Country of the user
  • Age: Age of the user
  • Gender: Gender of the user
  • Subscription_Status: User's subscription status (Free, Premium)
  • Ratings: User's rating for the content (1 to 5 scale)
  • Languages: Language of the content
  • Device_Type: Type of device used (e.g., Smartphone, Tablet)
  • Location: Location of the user
  • Playback_Quality: Quality of video playback (e.g., HD, SD, 4K)
  • Interaction_Events: Count of interaction events during the session

Data Quality

  • No duplicate entries were found in the dataset.
  • The dataset contains a variety of genres, including Comedy, Drama, Sci-Fi, Documentary, Action, and Thriller.
  • There are no missing values in any columns, ensuring data integrity.

Key Findings

1. User Ratings

The distribution of ratings indicates a predominance of lower scores: - Ratings (1 to 5 scale): - 1: 1,237 - 2: 1,242 - 3: 1,301 - 4: 1,230 - 5: 1,204

This suggests a need for content improvement to enhance user satisfaction.

2. Average Duration Watched by Genre

The average duration watched varies significantly by genre: - Action: 43.52 minutes - Documentary: 43.37 minutes - Thriller: 42.98 minutes - Comedy: 42.91 minutes - Sci-Fi: 42.59 minutes - Drama: 42.38 minutes

Action content holds the highest engagement, indicating its popularity among users.

3. Average Ratings by Genre

The average ratings show a slight variation among genres: - Action: 3.04 - Drama: 3.02 - Comedy: 3.01 - Thriller: 2.97 - Sci-Fi: 2.96 - Documentary: 2.93

Despite Action being the most engaging genre, its ratings are not significantly high, suggesting room for improvement.

4. Subscription Status

The distribution of subscription status is nearly equal: - Free Users: 3,111 - Premium Users: 3,103

This balance presents an opportunity to convert Free users to Premium subscribers through targeted marketing.

5. Average Age by Subscription Status

The average age of users across subscription types is similar: - Free: 39.52 years - Premium: 39.81 years

6. Device Type Distribution

The device type distribution shows that Smart TVs are the most commonly used devices: - Smart TV: 1,306 - Desktop: 1,270 - Smartphone: 1,233 - Tablet: 1,217 - Laptop: 1,188

This highlights the importance of optimizing content for Smart TV users.

7. Interaction Events

The total interaction events by genre indicate high user engagement: - Documentary: 54,926 events - Comedy: 53,803 events - Sci-Fi: 52,665 events - Action: 52,236 events - Thriller: 51,679 events - Drama: 50,218 events

8. Correlation Analysis

The correlations between interaction events, ratings, and duration watched are weak, suggesting that: - Interaction events do not significantly influence ratings or the duration watched.

9. Average Duration Watched by Age Group

The average duration watched increases slightly with age: - Age Group 51-60: 44.71 minutes (highest engagement)

Strategic Marketing Recommendations

Based on the insights derived from the data analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

  • Targeted Content Marketing: Focus on promoting new releases in the Action and Documentary genres to enhance user engagement.

  • Improving Ratings: Conduct user feedback sessions to understand content improvement areas, as ratings are generally low across genres.

  • Age-Specific Campaigns: Tailor marketing campaigns to target older demographics, particularly users in the 51-60 age range, who exhibit higher engagement.

  • Device-Specific Marketing: Optimize marketing efforts for Smart TV users, as they constitute the largest user group.

  • Leveraging Interaction Events: Promote similar content in the Documentary genre, which generates the most interaction events.

Conclusion

This analysis provides valuable insights into user behavior and preferences on the streaming platform. By implementing the recommended strategies, the streaming service can enhance user engagement, improve content quality, and potentially increase subscription conversions.
